| Term | Meaning | Example from the Show |

| **Red Tops / Yellow Tops** | Colored capsules containing heroin doses; color identifies supplier/batch | *"Let me get two red tops."* — S02E05 |

## ORGANIZATIONAL HIERARCHY (Based on slang usage)

1. **Connect / Supplier** — Top tier drug source
2. **Re-up Man** — Distribution coordinator
3. **Working Man / Hustler** — Street dealer
4. **Runner** — Product carrier
5. **Hopper** — Young street dealer / lookout
6. **Muscle** — Armed enforcer
7. **Fiend** — End consumer / addict

## KEY OBSERVATIONS

**Code & Communication**: Street slang serves as coded language to discuss illegal activity in mixed company, particularly avoiding police detection.

**Business Structure**: Terms reveal the drug trade operates like a legitimate business — with supply chains, product lines, marketing (testers), and territory management.

**Violence as Control**: Muscle, stick-up boys, and shooters show violence is central to maintaining operations and territorial claims.

**Generational**: "Hoppers" and "youngins" terminology shows the institutionalization of street trade across generations.

**Temporal Evolution**: Later seasons show new tech terms (burners = cell phones), reflecting technology advancement in the drug trade.

**Geography as Identity**: References to specific projects (The Pit, Franklin Terrace, low-rises) show how location defines gang/dealer organization.
